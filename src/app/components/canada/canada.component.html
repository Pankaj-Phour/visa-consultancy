7 Great Advantages for International Students in 2020

One of the most popular countries in the world, Canada, has emerged as one of the hottest options to study abroad for International students. There are several reasons why International students prefer Canada over other developed countries like USA, Australia, New Zealand etc. Some of the reasons are affordable tuition fees, cheap living cost, safe cities, ample of job options, work permit (both during and after the study period) and study leading as a pathway to permanent residency in Canada. Let’s discuss these reasons in the more elaborative manner :-

  • Top ranked Universities/ Colleges

    Canada is renowned in the world for its quality of education, research centres and innovation. Students have multiple options in terms of higher education programs in Canada. Students can choose according to their preference from 1 year diploma to 4 years degree programs. A Canadian degree, diploma or certificate is generally recognised as equivalent to other developed countries because of its high academic standards.

  • Affordable tuition fees

    Canada’s tuition fees are comparatively lower to other countries such as the USA, United Kingdom, Australia and New Zealand. Some programmes range between 1,000 and 4,000 CAD/year, but the majority of them cost over 10,000 CAD/ year. Canada’s living cost, entertainment and food cost is also cheaper than the USA and UK. That is the reason why students all around the globe are widely choosing Canada over any other country.

  • Canada’s student visa process

    Due to its transparent and easily accessible Visa Application process; Canada has become a huge attraction for international students. All around the world students want to apply to Top Universities and colleges of Canada. If you want to apply Bachelor’s degree at a Canadian university, all you need to do is upload the required documents :

    • Your +2 certificate
    • The completed application form
    • Your CV
    • Your Letter of Intent
    • IELTS TRF
    • Evidence that you can support yourself financially during your studies
  • Part- Time working hours

    One of the biggest advantages of study in Canada is the available working hours while studying. International students are allowed to work 20 hours a week and upto 30 hours during school breaks. This helps the students to manage their expenses without causing any financial burden on their families. Students, who choose to work in the campus area, do not need any work permit but in order to work off- campus; a student must acquire a work permit. Being an International student, If you want to apply for work permit in Canada; you must have following documents

    • Valid study permit;
    • be a full-time student
    • be enrolled at a designated learning institution at the post-secondary level or, in Quebec, a vocational program at the secondary level; and
    • be studying in an academic, vocational or professional training program that leads to a degree, diploma or certificate that is at least six months in duration.
  • Permanent Residency

    Students who have acquired Canadian work experience through Post-Graduate Work Permit Program; fall under Canadian Experience Class (CEC) to apply Permanent Residency in Canada.

    Candidates for British Columbia’s International Post-Graduate category have the advantage of not requiring a job offer and being able to have their application for permanent residence processed through the federal Express Entry immigration selection system. Students who graduate from a study program in Quebec get eligible to apply for a Quebec Selection Certificate (Certificat de sélection du Québec, commonly known as a CSQ) through the Quebec Experience Class.

  • Safest Country to live and thrive

    Canadians are known for their friendliness and welcoming nature. Canada has always invited International students with open arms for study as well as permanent residency. Canada is ranked at number 6 among the top 10 countries to live in 2020. Lower crime rates, amusing natural landscapes, multiculturalism, amazing job opportunities, student security and health beneficiary plans are some of the few reasons why students should highly consider Canada for their number one choice for study abroad.

  • High - tech Campus

    Canada has always stayed at the forefront to follow the technological trends. It has consistently emerged as an international leader in computer and information technology. Today, students in Canada are studying and learning in an advanced environment because of its SchoolNet program. Students can take advantage of high tech amenities that are available in Canadian colleges and Universities to learn and grow.
